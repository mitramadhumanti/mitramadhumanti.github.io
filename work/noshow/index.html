Appointment No-Show Reduction

A data-driven initiative to diagnose and reduce a 22% appointment no-show rate across 90+ facilities — combining predictive modelling, behavioural segmentation, and automated patient engagement to recover lost revenue and capacity.

The Problem

A 22% appointment no-show rate across the network was eroding revenue, misallocating staff capacity, and degrading service quality. A 12-month data audit combined with a 340-respondent patient survey established the baseline: forgetting accounted for 38% of no-shows, with the remainder split across transportation barriers, scheduling conflicts, and appointment-related anxiety — each requiring a fundamentally different intervention.

What I Delivered

  • 01
    Discovery & Root Cause Analysis
    • Ran stakeholder interviews, a 12-month historical data audit, and a 340-respondent patient survey to establish the 22% baseline no-show rate
    • Segmented root causes into forgetting, transportation, scheduling conflict, and anxiety-driven avoidance
  • 02
    Solution Design & Pilot Preparation
    • Designed five targeted interventions: multi-channel SMS/WhatsApp reminders, self-service one-click rescheduling, ML-based no-show risk scoring, automated waitlist backfill, and policy/incentive adjustments
    • Built the pilot protocol with a proper control group rather than a single uncontrolled rollout
  • 03
    A/B Pilot at Two Facilities
    • Ran a controlled pilot achieving a 13.4% no-show rate in the treatment group versus 22.1% in control — a 39% relative reduction
    • Same-day cancellations rose 77% in the treatment group, enabling real-time backfill
  • 04
    Phased Rollout Across Facilities
    • Rolled out in waves across 17 hospitals, 8 dental centres, 10 public health centres, 60 primary health care centres, and 2 physiotherapy units
  • 05
    Predictive Risk Scoring & Backfill Automation
    • Built a 50+ variable predictive model to score appointment-level no-show risk, concentrating proactive outreach where it had the highest expected return
    • Connected risk scoring to the waitlist backfill engine so high-risk slots were pre-emptively offered to waitlisted patients
  • 06
    Steady-State Monitoring
    • Stabilised the no-show rate at 11% post-rollout, with $480K in annual revenue recovered
    • Patient satisfaction (CSAT) rose from 3.8 to 4.3 out of 5

Retrospective

✓ What Went Well

  • Dual-touch reminders (48h + 24h) outperformed single reminders by 23%
  • One-click rescheduling converted no-shows into actionable cancellations, enabling real-time backfill
  • ML risk scoring concentrated proactive outreach where ROI was highest
  • Phased rollout gave each wave time to stabilise before the next began

⚠ Challenges

  • Change management was underestimated — staff buy-in lagged technical delivery by weeks
  • Transportation and anxiety-driven no-shows weren't meaningfully addressed by reminders alone
  • The ML model required 18 months of historical data, limiting generalisability to newer service lines

→ Key Lessons

  • Behavioural drivers require fundamentally different interventions than informational ones — segment before designing solutions
  • Friction removal matters more than message content
  • Staff are stakeholders, not just users — involve and train them in parallel with technical development
  • Design the full cancellation-to-backfill loop from day one
