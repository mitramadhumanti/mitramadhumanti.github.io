The Problem

Pre-authorization and claims workflows were fragmented across EHRs, payer portals, and manual document queues. Authorization specialists spent the bulk of their time on manual document review rather than judgement-intensive case work, while denial patterns and SLA bottlenecks across high-volume specialties went largely undiagnosed until they showed up as lost revenue.

What I Delivered

  • 01
    Discovery & Current-State Assessment
    • Mapped the end-to-end pre-authorization and claims workflow across the facility network
    • Identified high-volume specialties, denial patterns, SLA bottlenecks, and established baseline KPIs
  • 02
    Requirements, AI Model Design & Integration Planning
    • Designed the HL7/FHIR integration architecture, data dictionary, payer-rule engine logic, and NLP extraction model plan
  • 03
    MVP Build — Extraction, Validation & Risk Scoring
    • Shipped seven core modules: case intake, document extraction (OCR + NLP), eligibility validation, payer-rule engine, denial risk scoring, SLA-prioritised work queue, and operations dashboard
  • 04
    UAT & Compliance Sign-Off
    • Ran UAT with 18 authorization specialists across 3 facilities
    • Completed HIPAA controls review, audit trail validation, and AI governance sign-off
  • 05
    Pilot Launch — Selected Specialty & Payer Set
    • Launched with 3 specialties and 4 payers across 2 facilities with daily monitoring
  • 06
    Scale-Up Across Payers, Specialties & Facilities
    • Expanded to 9 payers and full specialty coverage across 90+ facilities
    • Added appeal packet generation and document follow-up automation in wave 2

Retrospective

✓ What Went Well

  • A configurable rules engine absorbed frequent payer rule changes without requiring code deploys
  • Human-in-the-loop review preserved clinical trust during early model adoption
  • Phased pilot by specialty reduced scope complexity and enabled genuinely useful UAT feedback
  • Denial risk scoring gave revenue cycle teams early warning before submission

⚠ Challenges

  • EHR and payer integration timelines were consistently underestimated
  • NLP model accuracy on handwritten and low-quality scanned documents was lower than expected
  • Compliance sign-off ran sequentially rather than in parallel with development, creating a three-week bottleneck
  • Clinical staff trust in AI recommendations built slowly — adoption lagged technical readiness

→ Key Lessons

  • Compliance review must run in parallel with development sprints
  • Model explainability is a clinical trust requirement, not a nice-to-have
  • Build the payer rules engine before the AI extraction layer
  • Data quality assessment must precede model training
  • Integration discovery consistently takes 2× longer than estimated — start immediately
