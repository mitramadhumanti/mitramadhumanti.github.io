Methodology

Every numeric score is derived from a named, dated public source — primarily KLAS Research reports, ONC/HealthIT.gov publications, vendor pricing pages, trade press (Fierce Healthcare, Healthcare IT News, TechTarget), and SEC/DOJ filings. Where a metric doesn't map cleanly to a single reported number (see conflicts below), it's built from a documented, transparent conversion — never invented from scratch — and flagged estimate rather than sourced in the matrix.

Scores are normalized to a 0–10 scale per criterion. The leaderboard score is a weighted average across all seven criteria, using whatever weights you set with the sliders.

Conflicts & judgment calls resolved

Market share isn't one number. Epic and Oracle Health are measured by KLAS on acute-care hospital contracts; athenahealth and Veradigm compete mainly in the ambulatory/outpatient market, which KLAS and Definitive Healthcare track separately and less granularly. There's no single index that puts all four on one scale, so this criterion is a flagged, analyst-normalized synthesis of both datasets rather than one clean citation.
Oracle Health and Veradigm interoperability scores are estimated. KLAS's "EHR Interoperability Overview 2025" published a 1–9 integration-goals score for Epic (8.0) and athenahealth (7.7), but didn't publish a directly comparable number for Oracle Health or Veradigm in the sources available. Their scores here are estimated from qualitative KLAS/vendor commentary (FHIR R4 support, CommonWell/Carequality participation, QHIN pursuit) and should be treated as directional, not a KLAS-published figure.
Veradigm's ambient-AI score is a low-confidence estimate. Veradigm acquired ScienceIO (an AI/foundation-model company) in March 2024, but public reporting shows little evidence of a deployed, clinician-facing ambient scribe at the scale of Epic's Art, athenahealth's athenaAmbient, or Oracle's Clinical AI Agent. Scored low and flagged accordingly rather than left blank.
KLAS letter grades were converted to a 0–10 scale. The KLAS "Complete Looks" series scores ambulatory vendors with letter grades (e.g. Veradigm's "D," 43% satisfied). Converting a letter grade to a decimal score is a judgment call, made transparently and flagged as such.
